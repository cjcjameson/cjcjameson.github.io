1. Check Your Pebbles

The Rocks, Pebbles, and Sand metaphor
(Alex Ponomarev)

Examples of Rocks for a Data Team

- Replatform to new warehouse, retire legacy
- Install new catalog & semantic layer
- Add Streaming to make things real-time
- Quality Initiative: tests, data contracts, and observability
- “Data Products” with end-user value

🌶️ Spicy Take: Reward autonomy in performance reviews ... not only Rocks 🌶️

2. Say What You Mean

There aren't enough Data Product Managers to tell you what to build. Every leader on a data team can take ownership and say what you mean.

By Career Track...

- Technical-Expert: Say how to build it!
- Analysts: Say what matters!
- Line-of-Business: Say what decisions are critical!
- Data PM: Say what is reusable & durable!
- Manager: Say where things could be better!
- Field/Solutions: Say it’s the right tool for the job!

"Fiery Leaders" by Eric Livingston
Salesforce's V2MOM Framework ... pick something like this and go for it!

🌶️ Spicy Take: It’s ok if 1:1s focus on the task at hand 🌶️

3. Find Your Want

Amid all the support your data team does for other teams, what's your personal drive? If you're building self-serve analytics, where's the self-care?
